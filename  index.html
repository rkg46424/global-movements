The System

How the hashtag
system works

The genius of this movement is making the justice gap visible with numbers. Every country gets a code. Every case gets counted. The gap between #Stop and #Justice becomes a public indictment of governments that fail their people.

For Rape

#StopNow [COUNTRY]#[CASES]
#JusticeNow [COUNTRY]#[CONVICTIONS]

Example: If India had 10,000 reported rape cases and justice was served in only 50 — post #StopNow IND#10000 alongside #JusticeNow IND#50 to make the gap undeniable.

For Human Trafficking

#StopNowHT [COUNTRY]#[CASES]
#JusticeNowHT [COUNTRY]#[CONVICTIONS]

Example: If China had 20,000 reported HT cases and only 20 convictions — post #StopNowHT CHI#20000 and #JusticeNowHT CHI#20 to force accountability.

COVID-19 Impact

The pandemic made it
dramatically worse

COVID-19 has severely exacerbated human trafficking. Traffickers exploited economic desperation, disrupted services, and enforcement gaps — making already vulnerable populations even more at risk. (Source: Global Protection Cluster Anti-Trafficking Task Team, 2020)

⚠

New victims created

Economic downturn and job losses created new targets. Traffickers offered "employment opportunities" to desperate displaced persons.

🔒

Victims trapped further

Trafficked persons were physically confined and unable to exit. Restrictions meant they could not access help, healthcare, or support services.

📉

Services collapsed

Clinics, shelters and support offices closed. Law enforcement resources were diverted, delaying justice for victims in the criminal system.

👧

Children at extreme risk

School closures and caregiver illness left children unattended. Some caregivers — under false promises of education — unknowingly handed children to traffickers.

💻

Exploitation moved online

Movement restrictions shifted exploitation to digital spaces. Women and children were commercially sexually exploited online and within private homes.

♀

Women & girls hardest hit

Gender inequality was amplified. Domestic violence surged. Internally displaced women and girls faced disproportionate risk of falling into trafficking.
